\chapter*{Abstract} %l'asterisco dopo chapter serve per visualizzare il capitolo come "non numerato"
Road pothole detection is a significant problem in the field of road maintenance. It is also a challenging task due to the variety of pothole shapes and sizes and it has been tackled with different approaches by the Computer Vision community. 

In this work, we propose an implementation of the Mask R-CNN architecture for the task of road pothole istance segmentation.
We show that the model is able to achieve good performance on the task, and we provide an analysis of the results obtained on the dataset used for the experiments: showing also that the main restriction is the lack of a large well labelled dataset and the ambiguity of the definition of pothole itself.
